# **L293D Motor Driver IC**

## Introduction

The L293D is a motor driver integrated circuit (IC) used to control the speed and direction of DC motors and stepper motors. 
Since microcontrollers like Arduino can only supply a small amount of current they cannot drive motors directly. 
The L293D solves this problem by acting as an interface between the microcontroller and the motor.
It contains two built-in H-bridge circuits which allow two motors to rotate in both forward and reverse directions. 
The IC also supports speed control using Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) and includes internal protection diodes to handle voltage spikes generated by motors.
Due to its simplicity, reliability, and wide voltage range the L293D is commonly used in robotics, automation and embedded system projects.

---

## Internal Architecture

The L293D consists of:

- Four high-current half-H drivers
- Two complete H-bridge configurations
- Internal clamp (flyback) diodes
- Separate logic and motor power supplies

### IC Technology Used

The L293D is built using Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT) technology

Advantages:
- High current gain
- Simple interface

Disadvantages:
- High saturation voltage (VCE(sat))
- Reduced efficiency compared to MOSFET-based drivers

---

## H-Bridge Operation

An H-bridge is a switching configuration that allows voltage to be applied across a load in either direction.
Each H-bridge consists of four switching transistors arranged in an “H” configuration.

### 3.1 Motor Direction Control Table

| EN | IN1 | IN2 | Motor State        |
|----|-----|-----|--------------------|
| 0  | X   | X   | Disabled           |
| 1  | 1   | 0   | Clockwise rotation |
| 1  | 0   | 1   | Counterclockwise   |
| 1  | 0   | 0   | Stop               |
| 1  | 1   | 1   | Brake              |

The L293D contains two such H-bridges enabling control of two motors simultaneously.

---

## Pulse Width Modulation (PWM)

Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) is used to control motor speed.
PWM is applied to the Enable pins of the IC.
Duty Cycle formula:

Duty Cycle = (TON / Ttotal) × 100%

- 0% duty cycle → Motor OFF
- 50% duty cycle → Medium speed
- 100% duty cycle → Full speed

The motor responds to the average voltage determined by the duty cycle.

Average Voltage = Duty Cycle × Motor Supply Voltage

---


## Thermal Considerations

Power Dissipation:

P = I × Vdrop

Example:
If I = 0.6A and Vdrop = 2V

P = 1.2W

Excessive power dissipation causes heating. Ground pins (4, 5, 12, 13) are internally connected to heat-dissipating regions to manage thermal load.

---

## Protection Mechanism

The “D” in L293D indicates the presence of internal clamp diodes.

When a motor is switched OFF suddenly, it generates a voltage spike known as Back EMF.

The internal diodes:
- Provide a safe discharge path
- Protect the IC from voltage damage
- Improve circuit reliability

## Advantages

- Dual H-bridge in a single IC
- Built-in flyback diodes
- Wide motor voltage range (up to 36V)
- Simple TTL-compatible interface
- Suitable for educational and robotics projects

---

## Limitations

- High voltage drop due to BJT technology
- Limited continuous current (600mA)
- Lower efficiency compared to MOSFET-based drivers
- Not ideal for high-power motors

---

## Applications

- DC motor control
- Stepper motor driving
- Robotics
- Automation projects
- Embedded motor control systems

---

## Conclusion

The L293D motor driver IC is a dual H-bridge driver designed for bidirectional control of DC motors using low-power logic signals. 
It supports PWM-based speed control and includes internal protection diodes for inductive load safety.
Although newer MOSFET-based motor drivers offer better efficiency and higher current capacity
the L293D remains a widely used and beginner-friendly solution for low-to-medium power motor control applications.
